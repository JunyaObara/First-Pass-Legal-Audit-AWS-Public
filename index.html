Governed Architecture, Not A Legal Engine

Built by Junya Obara as a self-initiated AWS GenAI PoC to demonstrate customer-backward problem framing, Amazon Bedrock Knowledge Bases RAG design, event-driven AWS architecture, Human-in-the-Loop governance, and evaluation-aware LLMOps.

This public site summarizes the architecture, evaluation approach, and responsible AI boundaries of the PoC. The implementation is treated as a governed software workflow, not as an autonomous legal decision engine.

The PoC demonstrates first-pass triage and evidence preparation. Production deployment would require additional enterprise identity, access control, monitoring, and operational hardening.

Customer value

Increase review coverage while reducing expert workload

For regulated companies releasing software globally, this PoC helps surface privacy and compliance issues during specification writing. It does not replace legal judgment; it reduces reviewer workload and late-stage rework.

AWS architecture

Cloud-native workflow

S3 and EventBridge decouple intake, Step Functions makes review and approval visible and retryable, Lambda isolates each stage, Bedrock Knowledge Bases grounds retrieval, and DynamoDB/S3 preserve state and evidence.

GenAI/RAG

RAG with legal provenance

Statute-first, law-scoped retrieval with separate statute and case-law lanes. Case-law support remains conditional for Legal review rather than the default source of truth.

Responsible AI

Human-in-the-Loop control

Engineering review is required, Legal review is conditional, and Business approval is required. AI prepares evidence and routing; humans remain accountable for decisions.

LLMOps

Release discipline

Validation stages, baseline metrics, release gates, and traceable release records keep quality claims tied to measurable evidence rather than demo-only impressions.

Security

Encryption and approval security

Core S3 buckets and prompt cache use SSE-KMS, DynamoDB workflow state is encrypted, approval signing material is stored in Secrets Manager, and CloudWatch plus optional CloudTrail support operational visibility and audit review.

Customer Problem

Global Software Delivery Creates A Compliance Review Bottleneck

Companies that ship products, platforms, apps, APIs, and data-driven features across markets often face privacy and compliance review across jurisdictions. Review can become a late-stage bottleneck when issues are discovered after specifications are already mature.

This PoC was motivated by the kind of review bottleneck that appears in Automotive / SDV delivery: frequent software specification changes, region-specific rollout constraints, privacy-sensitive connected services, and the need to coordinate engineering, legal, and business owners before launch.

The customer value is not automated legal judgment. The value is shifting review left: use GenAI to prepare a grounded first pass, surface ambiguous or high-risk requirements earlier, route the right items to the right owners, and reduce downstream rework while preserving human accountability.

Core Contract

  • Inputs are software-spec fragments and law scope.
  • Outputs are first-pass triage, evidence, routing, and recommendations.
  • External labels stay limited to BLOCKER, NEEDS_REVIEW, and CLEARED.
  • Final judgment remains Human-in-the-Loop.

AWS Architecture

Event-Driven GenAI Review Workflow

S3 and EventBridge decouple specification intake from orchestration. Step Functions makes audit, validation, and approval workflows visible and retryable. Lambda stages isolate validation, retrieval, verdicting, finalization, approval, and notification. Bedrock Knowledge Bases and OpenSearch Serverless support grounded retrieval; Bedrock Runtime supports selected LLM-assisted stages. DynamoDB, S3, SNS, API Gateway, KMS, CloudWatch, and optional CloudTrail provide state, evidence storage, approval integration, security controls, and observability.

Why This Architecture Matters

EventBridge decouples S3 ingestion from orchestration and keeps event payloads explicit.

Step Functions makes long-running audit, validation, and approval workflows visible, retryable, and explainable.

Lambda stage isolation keeps validation, retrieval, verdicting, approval, and notification boundaries inspectable.

Bedrock Knowledge Bases provide a managed statute-grounded retrieval path while preserving retrieval provenance.

S3 and DynamoDB preserve evidence artifacts, run state, approval tasks, and idempotency state.

KMS, CloudWatch, SNS, and API Gateway support encryption posture, observability, notifications, and review workflow integration.

Why Bedrock KB

Grounding changes faster than the model

Legal and policy sources change by jurisdiction and domain. Keeping them in a retrieval layer makes citations, updates, and expansion easier than relying on a foundation model's internal knowledge.

Why not model-only

Answers need reviewable evidence

A direct model answer can be quick, but reviewers need to inspect the statute basis. RAG gives the workflow a concrete evidence object to store, evaluate, and route.

Why not fine-tune first

The first risk is provenance, not style

Fine-tuning may help fixed patterns later, but this PoC needs law updates, law-scope filters, and citation-backed triage before optimizing model behavior.

GenAI/RAG Design

LLM-Assisted, Contract-Controlled Pipeline

The AI path decomposes compliance triage into inspectable stages. The LLM assists selected extraction and planning tasks; deterministic contracts and evidence rules control final routing.

Spec input Checklist / issue extraction Query planning Law-scoped statute retrieval Basis selection / evidence sufficiency Deterministic adjudication Recommendations Citation projection Conditional case-law support for Legal review only Structured audit result
LLM assists selected stages; deterministic contracts and evidence rules control final routing.
Why checklist extraction

Turn ambiguous specs into reviewable issues

The system first maps a free-form specification into law-specific anchors, evidence classes, and checklist states so later stages have explicit inputs.

Why law-scoped retrieval

Prevent cross-law evidence drift

Queries are scoped by law and evidence lane so a VPPA row does not casually borrow CIPA evidence, and statute grounding remains primary.

Why deterministic adjudication

Keep final routing testable

The LLM can help with extraction and planning, but outcome labels, review routes, weak-evidence handling, and recommendations are controlled by deterministic contracts.

Why case-law is conditional

Support Legal review without replacing statutes

Case-law retrieval is separated and used as Legal-review support when enabled; it is not treated as the default primary evidence source.

Evaluation & Release Governance

LLMOps Discipline Beyond A Demo

The PoC treats evaluation as a release decision, not as an after-the-fact demo score. Candidate changes are compared against defined validation conditions, baseline metrics, and release gates before stronger claims are made.

Held-out data is kept separate from day-to-day tuning. CI checks support repeatable governance checks, but they are not presented as proof of live AWS health.

Validation Ladder

  2. Targeted slice
  3. Random slice
  4. Full validation
  5. Held-out confirmation as final gate
